Hiring Managers to have both a Hiring Committee and Advisory Committee made up of 3-4 people on each committee. Here’s some insight to what that should look like:

Hiring Committee Advisory Committee/Stakeholders
Role Central to the candidate selection process. Comprises individuals who will closely interact with the new hire.
Focus Creating a positive experience for all candidates and ensuring competency-based assessments. Contributing insights and feedback based on specific focus areas.
Responsibilities Recommends finalists, with the Hiring Manager making the final decision, backed by thorough rationale. While their input is valuable, decision-making rests with the Hiring Committee to maintain process integrity and clarity.

Competencies: These are a combination of critical skills necessary for a candidate to be successful in the role. Interviews are structured around these competencies to collect evidence that supports (or not) the likelihood of success.

  • Behavioral Competencies: These are behaviors that are critical for success in the role. They are HOW someone will operate in the role. Ex. communication, collaboration, customer focus.
  • Functional Competencies: These are the functional and/or technical ("hard") skills. These are WHAT a candidate will do in the role. Ex. business development, accounting.
